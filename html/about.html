Early Life

Michael Jeffrey Jordan was born on February 17, 1963, in Brooklyn, New York. He grew up in Wilmington, North Carolina, and quickly developed a passion for basketball.

Education

He attended the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, where he played college basketball and led his team to a national championship in 1982.

Career

Jordan was drafted by the Chicago Bulls in 1984. He won six NBA championships and five MVP awards. He is widely regarded as the greatest basketball player of all time.
